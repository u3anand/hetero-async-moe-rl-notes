# R3

[Source](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.11370)

Within-replica train/inference router alignment. Foundation that [[Paper Direction|the proposed direction]] extends to cross-replica.

## Core mechanism

Records binary expert-selection mask from inference engine during rollout; replays it during training forward pass. Training logits still computed normally (router still gets gradient), but expert selection forced to match inference.

## Why it matters here

- Confirms routing distribution as load-bearing source of MoE RL instability.
- Reports: even with identical weights, numerical differences cause ~10% router disagreement per forward pass; 94% of tokens differ in at least one layer.
- Defines the within-replica problem cleanly → our cross-replica extension has a clean baseline to differentiate against.

## What it does NOT address

- Multiple async rollout replicas at different staleness.
- Cross-replica routing distribution divergence.
- Heterogeneous clusters.

## Systems Lens
- Workload: MoE RL fine-tuning (slime/Megatron-style).
- Bottleneck: train/inference routing KL → training collapse.
- Scheduling / placement: N/A (algorithm-level).
- Communication: extra routing-mask payload rollout→train.
- Heterogeneous cluster angle: none.
- Relevance to MoE RL / SWE-RL: foundational.
